The Peters Lab will be opening in the Oxford University Department of Physiology, Anatomy and Genetics

We're interested in the relationship between brain activity and behavior, and study how activity flows between regions and changes with learning to guide movement.

In particular, we're focused on the loop between the cortex, basal ganglia, and thalamus. These structures work together to learn and execute behavior, but we don't have a good understanding of how they do this.

We investigate these questions using the full modern toolbox for systems neuroscience to record and manipulate activity in mice performing simple tasks, including:

Widefield imaging Neuropixels electrophysiology Optogenetics Two-photon imaging
