We're a brand-new lab looking for a team that's excited about neuroscience, focused on putting out good research, and ready to help establish our science from the ground-up.

There are a lot of unique rewards and challenges in joining a new lab, since starting members will help get the lab up and running. I'm dedicated towards making this a collaborative process: I want my trainees to build good foundations and accomplish their invidual goals, and I want to continually improve my mentorship.

Projects can be flexible related to the themes of the lab:

  • Interactions between the cortex, basal ganglia, and thalamus
  • Changes in activity over learning
  • Mechanisms of behavior/action/motor control
If you're interested in joining the lab, feel free to send me an informal email.

Postdocs

Postdocs would ideally have experimental experience in electrophysiology or imaging, substantial coding experience, and a previously published first-author paper. The goal for postdocs is to come in ready to do science, and for me to help them publish successful projects and hit their career objectives. We'll plan on starting postdoctoral fellowship applications before you arrive to develop project ideas, help build your CV, and contribute some money to the lab. I likely can't accept a postdoc before ~October 2023 without a fellowship, but I can accept a postdoc with a fellowship any time.

Doctoral students

Doctoral students don't need extensive experience, though lab and coding experience is ideal. The goal for doctoral students is to come in ready to learn, and for me to help them develop the skills to be a scientist. We can take students from these doctoral programs:
  • DPhil in Neuroscience (1yr MSc + 3yr DPhil)
  • DPhil by Reserach in Physiology, Anatomy, and Genetics
  • DPhil by Reserach in Physiology, Anatomy, and Genetics

Masters students

Masters students don't need experience, just excitment about starting out doing research. The goal is for masters students to accomplish a small project that gives them a realistic experience of how science is done.

Undergraduate students

We may be able to take in undergraduate students to get exposure to research and (depending on what's going on in the lab) help out with projects. Undergraduates can shadow (short-term) or work with a student or postdoc in the lab who would benefit from extra help, so availability depends on current projects.
